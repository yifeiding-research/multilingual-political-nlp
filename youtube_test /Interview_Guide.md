# Political Influencer Interview Guide
## Three-Country Comparative Study

### Interview Structure (60-90 minutes)

**Part 1: Background (10 min)**
**Part 2: Content Strategy (20 min)**
**Part 3: Platform & Audience (15 min)**
**Part 4: Political Environment (15 min)**
**Part 5: Future & Reflections (10 min)**

---

## PART 1: Background & Motivation

### General Questions (All Countries)

1. How did you start creating political content on YouTube?
   - When did you start?
   - What motivated you to focus on politics?

2. How do you define your role?
   - Journalist? Activist? Educator? Entertainer? Commentator?
   - How do you see yourself differently from traditional media?

3. What is your political stance/affiliation?
   - Independent? Affiliated with a party?
   - How does this influence your content?

---

## PART 2: Content Strategy

### Language Strategy (Luxembourg-Specific)

**FOR LUXEMBOURG INFLUENCERS:**

4. Which languages do you use in your videos?
   - Luxembourgish, French, German, English?
   - Why these specific languages?

5. How do you decide which language to use for each video?
   - Topic-based? Audience-based? Platform-based?
   - Do you translate or create separate versions?

6. What are the challenges of multilingual political communication?
   - Does it limit your reach?
   - Does it fragment your audience?
   - How do different language communities react?

7. Do you feel pressure to choose certain languages over others?
   - From audience? Platforms? Political actors?

### Content Production (All Countries)

8. How do you decide what topics to cover?
   - News events? Audience requests? Personal interests?
   - How often do you post?

9. How do you balance different content types?
   - Criticism vs. education vs. entertainment?
   - Partisan vs. neutral content?

10. Do you ever avoid certain topics or angles?
    - If yes, why? (Controversial? Complex? Risky?)
    - If no, has anything happened as a result?

---

## PART 3: Platform & Audience

### Platform Choice

11. Why YouTube specifically?
    - Why not Instagram, TikTok, X, or traditional media?
    - Do you use other platforms? How do they differ?

12. How does YouTube's algorithm affect your work?
    - Does it favor certain content types?
    - Do you adjust content for better reach?
    - Have you experienced shadow-banning or demonetization?

### Audience Engagement

13. Who is your target audience?
    - Demographics? Political leanings? Geographic location?
    - How well do you know your actual audience?

14. How do you interact with your audience?
    - Comments? Community posts? Live streams?
    - Do you adjust content based on feedback?

15. What is your relationship with your audience?
    - Do you feel accountable to them?
    - How do they influence your editorial choices?

### Reach & Impact

16. How do you measure success?
    - Views? Engagement? Political impact? Revenue?
    - What metrics matter most to you?

17. **(Small Country Specific - Luxembourg)**
    Do you feel limited by Luxembourg's small population?
    - Have you considered targeting broader audiences?
    - How does market size affect your sustainability?

---

## PART 4: Political Environment

### Freedom & Constraints (All Countries)

18. Do you feel free to criticize political authorities?
    - Are there any red lines?
    - Have you faced any consequences for your content?

19. Have you experienced any pressure from:
    - Government officials?
    - Political parties?
    - Advertisers or sponsors?
    - Platform companies?
    - Your audience?

### Democratic Quality Perception

20. How would you characterize the media environment in [your country]?
    - Free? Pluralistic? Polarized? Controlled?
    - How has it changed in recent years?

21. **(Hungary-Specific)**
    How has the political climate changed since 2010?
    - Do you feel more or less free to criticize government?
    - Have you adjusted your content strategy as a result?
    - Why do you use YouTube vs. traditional media?

22. **(France/Luxembourg-Specific)**
    Do you feel that political influencers have the same legitimacy as traditional journalists?
    - Are you treated differently by political actors?
    - Do you face different expectations or pressures?

### Comparison

23. Are you aware of political influencers in other countries?
    - Do you see differences in how they operate?
    - What do you think enables or constrains their work?

---

## PART 5: Monetization & Sustainability

### Revenue Model

24. How do you sustain your work financially?
    - YouTube ads? Sponsorships? Donations? Patreon?
    - Other income sources?

25. Does your revenue model affect your editorial independence?
    - Do you decline certain sponsors?
    - Do advertisers influence your content?

26. **(Small Country - Luxembourg)**
    Is it financially viable to be a full-time political influencer in Luxembourg?
    - Do you need other income sources?
    - Would you be more successful in a larger market?

---

## PART 6: Future & Reflections

27. How do you see your role evolving?
    - Will you continue? Expand? Professionalize?
    - What are your goals?

28. What would you change about the current media/political environment?
    - Platform policies? Media regulations? Political culture?

29. What advice would you give to someone starting as a political influencer today?

30. Is there anything else you'd like to add about your experience?

---

## PROBING QUESTIONS (Use as needed)

### For Vague Answers:
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "What exactly do you mean by [term]?"
- "How did that work in practice?"

### For Hypothesis Testing:

**H1 (Language):**
- "Do you ever code-switch within a single video? Why/why not?"
- "Have you noticed that certain languages get more engagement?"

**H2 (Democratic Quality):**
- "Have you ever self-censored? Can you describe a situation?"
- "Do you know influencers who have faced consequences for their content?"
- "How do you decide what is 'safe' vs 'risky' to say?"

**H3 (Small State):**
- "Do you feel you have a closer relationship with your audience than influencers in larger countries?"
- "Does being in a small country make it easier or harder to have political impact?"

## INTERVIEW PROTOCOL

### Before Interview:
1. Review influencer's channel (top 10 videos, recent content)
2. Note their subscriber count, typical engagement, posting frequency
3. Prepare country/channel-specific probes
4. Secure informed consent (record? Anonymous? Quote?)

### During Interview:
1. Build rapport - acknowledge their work
2. Start with easy questions (background)
3. Move to sensitive topics gradually
4. Use their own language/terms when possible
5. Allow tangents - they often reveal important insights
6. Take notes on non-verbal cues (hesitation, emotion)

### After Interview:
1. Transcribe within 48 hours
2. Send key quotes for verification (if agreed)
3. Code for themes: language strategy, self-censorship, platform choice, audience relationship
4. Compare across countries

---

## ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Informed Consent:
- Explain research purpose
- Right to refuse questions
- Right to withdraw
- How data will be used
- Anonymity options (especially for Hungary)

### Sensitive Topics:
- Self-censorship (implies fear)
- Political stance (could be used against them)
- Revenue details (personal financial info)

**For Hungary:**
- Offer complete anonymity
- Meet in secure location
- Don't record if they prefer
- Be aware of potential surveillance concerns

### Luxembourg-Specific: Luxembourgish Language Use

**[ONLY FOR LUXEMBOURG INFLUENCERS - CRITICAL FOR H5]**

11a. "I noticed that most Luxembourg political content on YouTube uses German or English rather than Luxembourgish. Can you help me understand why?"
   - Probe: Is this a conscious choice or just natural?
   - Probe: Do you think about which language to use for titles vs content?

11b. "Have you ever used Luxembourgish in video titles or descriptions?"
   - If YES: "Did you notice any difference in viewership or engagement?"
   - If NO: "What prevents you from using Luxembourgish?"

11c. "Do you think YouTube's algorithm treats Luxembourgish differently than other languages?"
   - Probe: Have you heard other creators talk about this?
   - Probe: Have you tested it yourself?

11d. "When you choose to write in German, French, or English instead of Luxembourgish, is it because:"
   - You want to reach more people?
   - You think YouTube will recommend it more?
   - Your audience prefers those languages?
   - It's just the professional norm in politics?
   - Other reasons?

11e. "If YouTube's algorithm treated all languages equally—if Luxembourgish got the same visibility as English or German—would you use it more often?"
   - Probe: How much more?
   - Probe: For which types of content?

11f. "Do you feel there's a tension between using Luxembourgish to represent Luxembourg identity versus using other languages to reach more people?"
   - Probe: How do you personally resolve this tension?
